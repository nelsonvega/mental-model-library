## 10 Hybrid Mental Models


***

### 1. Outcome Payoff Matrix

**Mixing technique:** Crossover Canvas — swap outputs of *Incentive Design (payoff matrices)*  with *Outcome > Activity*[^2][^3]

**Model:** Every feature is scored by the payoff it delivers to a *real transformation*, not just an in-app action. The payoff matrix axes are: **short-term engagement** vs. **long-term outcome achieved** (skill earned, revenue gained, time saved). Dominant strategy = actions that appear in both high-engagement AND high-outcome quadrants only get built.

**KPI:** % of active users who hit a measurable transformation milestone (e.g., shipped feature, saved X hours) within 30 days of joining — target ≥ 40%.

***

### 2. Reputation Compounding Loop

**Mixing technique:** SCAMPER Combine — *Repeated Games \& Reputation*  combined with *Feedback Loops*[^3][^2]

**Model:** Every contribution (answer, template, code snippet) starts a visible reputation clock. Reputation decays slowly without new contributions but compounds when others build on your work. The loop is: **contribute → get cited → reputation compounds → unlock new contribution rights**. No lurking equilibrium is sustainable.

**KPI:** Median time for a user to receive their first "compound event" (someone building on their contribution) — target ≤ 14 days for 60% of contributors.

***

### 3. Guild Jobs-to-be-Done

**Mixing technique:** Forced Association Grid — *Cooperative Competition (guilds)*  × *Jobs-to-be-Done*[^2][^3]

**Model:** Each guild is formed around a single, named job ("ship a construction bid template in 60 days"), not a topic or interest. Guild health is measured by how many members complete that job, not by activity volume. This kills vanity guilds and forces community design around real outcomes.

**KPI:** Guild job-completion rate — % of guild members who complete the stated job within the defined window; target ≥ 50% per cohort.

***

### 4. Scaffolded Fitness Frontier

**Mixing technique:** Crossover Canvas — *Scaffolded Difficulty*  mapped onto *Fitness Frontier (Pareto evolution)*[^1][^2]

**Model:** Difficulty levels are not static tracks but evolving Pareto fronts. As a user progresses, the system surfaces challenges that trade off **depth vs. speed vs. community contribution** — making the tradeoff visible. Users choose their Pareto point, and the system adapts content, quests, and peer matches accordingly.

**KPI:** User-reported challenge satisfaction score (1–5) at each level transition; target average ≥ 4.0, with < 10% drop-off at any single level boundary.

***

### 5. Public Goods Provocation Engine

**Mixing technique:** SCAMPER + Provocation Reversal  applied to *Public Goods \& Tragedy of the Commons*[^3][^1]

**Model:** Flip the assumption: instead of rewarding contribution to keep commons alive, **provocatively restrict access** to shared resources (templates, knowledge base) unless a user has contributed recently. The "tragedy" becomes the motivator — deprivation of commons is the provocation, and re-contribution restores access and status.

**KPI:** Commons Contribution Rate — % of monthly active users who make at least one qualifying contribution per 30-day period; target ≥ 35%.

***

### 6. Network Effect Cost-of-Inaction Signal

**Mixing technique:** Forced Association Grid — *Network Effects*  × *Cost of Inaction (COI)*, amplified by *Mechanism Design*[^2][^3]

**Model:** Make the cost of NOT being in the network explicit and personalized: show each user what capabilities, connections, and shortcuts they are missing by remaining passive. The network's Metcalfe value is visualized as a "gap score" — the delta between their current contribution state and a fully networked peer.

**KPI:** Gap Score Reduction Rate — average decrease in a user's gap score over 60 days post-onboarding; target ≥ 30% gap closure.

***

### 7. Narrative Identity Value Stream

**Mixing technique:** Pattern Template Transfer  — extract *Narrative Framing \& Identity*, instantiate into *Value Stream Thinking*[^1][^3][^2]

**Model:** Map the entire user journey as a story arc: **Stranger → Seeker → Practitioner → Mentor → Legend**. Each stage has a named identity, a specific value-stream milestone (first win, first transformation, first peer success), and a visible status marker. Churn is framed narratively as "abandoning your arc" — making leaving emotionally costly.

**KPI:** Story Arc Progression Rate — % of users advancing at least one identity stage per month; target ≥ 25% of monthly actives.

***

### 8. Weakness Breeder Onboarding

**Mixing technique:** Weakness Breeder  crossed with *Frictionless Onboarding*[^1][^2]

**Model:** Instead of a generic onboarding path, collect the specific failure mode that caused the user's previous tools or attempts to fail ("I couldn't stay consistent," "I didn't know where to start"). Onboarding is then generated *specifically for that failure mode*, turning the user's known weakness into the product's entry point and proof of value.

**KPI:** First-session "Aha Moment" rate — % of new users completing a meaningful, personalized first action tied to their stated weakness within the first session; target ≥ 60%.

***

### 9. Tit-for-Tat Discovery Engine

**Mixing technique:** SCAMPER Adapt — *Tit-for-tat \& Reciprocity*  adapted into *Continuous Discovery*[^3][^2]

**Model:** The product continuously surfaces user feedback opportunities using a reciprocity engine: the more insight a user shares (micro-surveys, reaction data, feature votes), the more they unlock personalized discovery features (early access, custom recommendations, curated cohorts). The game theory is explicit — insight given = insight received.

**KPI:** Discovery Reciprocity Score — ratio of users who both give and receive personalized value from the loop; target ≥ 2.5x engagement uplift vs. non-participating users.

***

### 10. Curriculum of Complexity × Flow Balance

**Mixing technique:** Crossover Canvas — *Curriculum of Complexity*  swapped into *Flow \& Challenge-Skill Balance*[^3][^1]

**Model:** The product's full learning and feature roadmap is itself a curriculum — from trivial MVP features (easy wins) to wildly ambitious capabilities (advanced mastery). Each tier funds and trains the next, and users' challenge-skill state is continuously monitored. When a user enters a flow state (success rate ~70–85%), the system accelerates their path to the next complexity tier rather than letting them plateau.

**KPI:** Flow-State Tier Graduation Rate — % of users in a flow state who advance to the next complexity tier within 21 days; target ≥ 45%, with a retention rate ≥ 80% post-graduation.
