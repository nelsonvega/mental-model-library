# top mental models for blue ocean strategy

Here's a comprehensive breakdown of the top mental models for Blue Ocean Strategy — check out the full report for deep dives into each framework.

The BOS toolkit, developed by W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne at INSEAD, gives you **9 core mental models** that work as a system:[^1]


| \# | Mental Model | What It Does |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 1 | **Value Innovation** | The foundational concept — pursue differentiation *and* low cost simultaneously[^2] |
| 2 | **Strategy Canvas** | Visualizes your value curve vs. competitors to expose red ocean groupthink[^3] |
| 3 | **Four Actions / ERRC Grid** | Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create — redesigns your value curve[^4] |
| 4 | **Six Paths Framework** | 6 lenses for finding where new market space can be created[^5] |
| 5 | **Three Tiers of Noncustomers** | Focuses on the *much larger* population not yet buying from your industry[^6] |
| 6 | **Buyer Utility Map** | 6×6 matrix of the full buyer experience — spots friction and whitespace[^7] |
| 7 | **Price Corridor of the Mass** | Sets pricing to capture the *mass* of new buyers, not just early adopters[^8] |
| 8 | **Pioneer-Migrator-Settler Map** | Portfolio diagnostic — which of your products are truly market-creating?[^9] |
| 9 | **Adjacent Models** | First Principles, JTBD, Disruptive Innovation — amplifiers for BOS thinking[^10] |

The highest-leverage starting point for a SaaS builder like yourself is usually the **Three Tiers of Noncustomers** combined with the **Six Paths Framework** — they force you off the customer-retention treadmill and toward discovering the *real* unserved market. From there, the **ERRC Grid** and **Strategy Canvas** let you design and visualize the alternative offering.[^11]
