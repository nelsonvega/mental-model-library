## Overview

This repository is a structured library of mental models organized across four domains:

| Domain | Models | Focus |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Product Design** | 47 models | Gamification, community engagement, habit formation, retention |
| **Execution & Strategy** | 20 models | Translating any mental model into an actionable plan |
| **Reverse Marketing** | 20 models | Making customers seek you out instead of chasing them |
| **Creative Synthesis** | 20 models | Combining ideas across domains to generate novel frameworks |

Every model includes practical application steps, real-world examples, KPI targets, and references to foundational books and research. This is not a theoretical exercise — it is a working playbook designed to be applied directly to product and go-to-market decisions.

## Document Guide

### 1. Gamification & Game Theory Models
**File:** `20 mental models to build community_educationa_sof.md`

20 mental models rooted in game theory and gamification for designing community, educational, and SaaS products. Organized into four groups:

| Group | Models | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Incentives, Payoffs & Cooperation** (1-5) | Payoff matrices, repeated games, tit-for-tat, public goods, cooperative competition | Structuring rational incentives for collaboration over exploitation |
| **Learning, Motivation & Progress** (6-10) | Zone of proximal development, progress loops, variable rewards, mastery paths, scaffolded autonomy | Keeping users engaged through adaptive challenge and visible growth |
| **Social Dynamics & Identity** (11-15) | Social identity, signaling, network effects, narrative identity, role differentiation | Building belonging, status, and tribal identity within the product |
| **Governance & Sustainability** (16-20) | Mechanism design, common-pool resource management, anti-fragile communities, emergent gameplay, meta-game evolution | Ensuring long-term community health and self-governance |

Includes a quick mapping table connecting each model to specific product design artifacts (onboarding flows, reward systems, moderation policies, etc.).

### 3. Addictive Product Design Foundations
**File:** `building-addictive-software-products-mm.md`

7 core mental models for ethical, habit-forming product design with deep references:

1. **Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)** — Design around the life "job" users hire your product to do
2. **Causal / Feedback Loops** — Map trigger-action-reward-investment cycles
3. **Value Stream / Time-to-Aha** — Minimize time from intent to first value moment
4. **Pareto & Power Users** — Identify the 20% of flows driving 80% of retention
5. **Kano Model** — Distinguish must-haves, performance features, and delighters
6. **Product-Market Fit** — Measure fit as a threshold, not a spectrum
7. **Pioneers, Settlers & Town Planners** — Wardley's evolution model for feature lifecycle

Each model includes recommended books, libraries, tools, and ethical frameworks for responsible implementation.

### 4. Execution Frameworks
**File:** `execution-mental-models.md`

20 execution-oriented mental models paired with a **Universal Application Framework** — a 4-step loop for applying any mental model to any target:

1. **Define the Target** — What specific outcome, problem, or decision?
2. **Map the Model** — How does the model's core logic describe your situation?
3. **Generate the Plan** — What specific actions does the model prescribe?
4. **Set Triggers & Measures** — What signals tell you it's working?

Models span six categories:
- **Systems & Causality:** First principles, second-order effects, feedback loops, constraint theory, emergence
- **Decision-Making:** Inversion, Bayesian updating, regret minimization, opportunity cost, pre-mortem
- **Prioritization:** Eisenhower matrix, Pareto, leverage points, ICE scoring
- **Cognitive Models:** Circle of competence, map vs. territory, Dunning-Kruger awareness
- **Behavioral Change:** Nudge architecture, habit loops, commitment devices
- **Strategic Planning:** OODA loop, scenario planning, Wardley mapping

- A markdown viewer (VS Code, GitHub, Obsidian, Notion, or any text editor)

## Contributing

Contributions that add new mental models, improve existing ones, or add verified real-world applications are welcome.

### Guidelines

1. **Follow the existing structure** — Each model should include: name, concise definition, application steps, and real-world examples
2. **Cite sources** — Use footnote references (`[^n]`) linking to books, papers, or verified case studies
3. **Include KPIs** — Where possible, attach measurable indicators to each model
4. **Verify applications** — Real-world examples should reference verifiable outcomes, not hypotheticals
5. **Keep it concise** — Each model should be understandable in under 2 minutes of reading

### Adding a New Model

```markdown
## Model Name

**Category:** [Incentives | Learning | Social | Governance | Execution | Marketing | Creative]

Description of the model in 2-3 sentences — what it is and why it matters.[^n]

**Application:**
1. Step one
2. Step two
3. Step three

**Real-world example:** Company X applied this model and achieved Y outcome.[^n]

**KPI:** Metric name — target ≥ X%.
```
